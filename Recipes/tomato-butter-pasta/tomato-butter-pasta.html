Fettuccine with cherry tomato butter sauce

Image from Sestrjevitovschii Ina on Unsplash

Description

Bright cherry tomatoes sauteed in butter with chopped onion, tossed with al dente fettuccine, and finished with fresh parsley and basil. The tomato butter sauce has an incredibly rich body and a velvety texture, making it the most underrated combination. Ever.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium onion diced
  • 3 pints cherry tomatos halved or 14 oz can of diced tomato
  • 1 pound fettuccine or linguine, spaghetti, etc
  • Roughly 100g of unpasteurised salted butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsely or basil

Instructions

  1. Saut√© the onion in butter in a large pan on medium heat for approximately 5 minutes or until onions become soft and translucent.
  2. Add tomatoes and break them down using a large spoon.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Cook the sauce over medium heat for 30 minutes. Stir frequently and lower heat if scorching. Bring the sauce to a simmer until preferred consistency.
  5. In a separate large pot, heat water to boiling point with 2 tbsp salt.
  6. Add fettuccine to boiling water and cook until al dente.
  7. Add the pasta to the sauce and toss to coat. If sauce is too dry, add reserved pasta water a few ounces at a time to loosen it up.
  8. Season to taste with more salt and pepper as required
  9. Garnish with coriander or parsley, then plate.
  10. Serve with grated parmesan cheese or any cheese of choice.

Storing Leftovers

Leftovers can be refrigerated for up to 3 days and can be reheated in the microwave.
